Healthy Habits

There's never been a better time to build healthy and long-lasting habits. We can all emerge from this crisis stronger and better.

Meditate

Meditation trains a part of your brain that's responsible for willpower and self control. This means you are able to control better your emotions, you can focus better and longer. Also provides a sense of general well-being, really important nowadays

Read a book

Reading financial education books would give you a better understanding about the current world situation. It would teach you about the best things you can do to improve your financial situation or any other area of your life.

Exercise

Who doesn't want to look sexy and fit, even in pajamas and wearing crocs. Exercise at home can be a great way to relief all the tension and uncertainty you may be feeling right now. Your overall mood will be a lot better

Cooking

Remember that advice about eating as healthy as you can? Well if you are able to make your own meals, having a good diet will be a piece of cake, and you can save some money too. You can also have a great time cooking

Learn something new

Getting into something new is always fun, you can enjoy discovering things you love doing. It could be playing an instrument, painting, make sick-looking drawings, or getting a new skill that can get you some money. More on that later

Sharpening Yourself

Pick up a new skill that could make you money even during this period of uncertainty. Something that is recession-proof and could be done remotely. And you know what recession is because you have been reading about it, don't you?

Disclaimer:

These are for informational purposes only. I recommend to build two to three habits at once even if you have time for more. Prioritize the habits that can help you the most right now.

Because even though the world is on pause, you don't have to be
